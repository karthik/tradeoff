  • Discussion
    Although ecologists have been well aware of the existence of individual heterogeneity, especially within groups of seemingly identical individuals, such grouping can still do a poor job of capturing the inherent variation. In this paper we demonstrate how the consideration of individual variation in classic life-history tradeoffs can result in dramatically different outcomes of trade-offs.
